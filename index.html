Skills Fields

Editing / 3D Modeling

I used to be in charge of the almost entire production process on some projects. It's why I had the occasion to work on video editing and 3D modeling.

For sure this fields are not my speciality, but they cross or approach my main skills such as motion design.
It also give me a pretty good ability in collaborating with video editors and 3D artists / modelers. I can easily fit in a team which is pretty well film-production-oriented.

Skills Fields

Research and development

Some projects require pushing the technology into uncharted territory. I can explore this, help to define the technical limits and if agreed upon, integrate the results of experimentation to the main production.

Also, it just so happens that I developed my own tools to simplify the more tedious steps we had to deal with on particular projects.

For instance, on I Kill A Friend and I Kill A Friend 2, [links] I developed a tool to check the position of the 3d mesh in space, the synch movement with the movie, the quality of the rotoscoping, and setup of the 3D lighting.
Likewise, on 208 Greetings [Links], I built an app to record the animation display of the characters which are dynamically generated.

Skills Fields

Compositing

Depending on the source material, I'm pretty comfortable with the following techniques: keying, rotoscoping and 2D/3D match moving.

I often aimed interactive video and movie synchronization with personal data, such as facebook profile, uploaded pictures... So I have a good knowledge of the different ways to export, integrate videos (and tracking data when needed), then finally drive them by programming.

I used to deal with quite bad quality footages and spend hours and days in retouching films frame by frame.
To avoid tedious work and poor results I can advice and request good practices before the film shoot. I'm also open to try each new technique wich can improve the workflow and the quality of the post-production process.

Skills Fields

Motion Design

I'm deeply interested on each motion graphic technique. You know, not only the different way to create a tween, generate spritesheets or gather bunch of keyframes and ease equations in any software.

I'd rather mean the way to make sense via movement and contribute to the mood in a project. I like to find the appropriate rythm or the right camera movement and try to offer the best transitions and rollovers to fit with the art director's vision.

I'm familiar with programmed, timeline based, both mixed and/or synchronized 2D and 3D motion graphics. Prerender or in realtime.

I know particle systems (Trapcode Particular, the built-in engine in Away3D 4 for example) and used to work a bit with physics engine.
Definitely not afraid to figure out and learn more.
